SOLOTOURER

Designed a community-driven web platform for female solo travelers in Brazil, enabling them to connect, share experiences, and support each other. By fostering a safe and interactive space, the platform encourages connection, knowledge sharing, and empowerment among solo female travelers.

ROLE

Research & UI/UX Design

TIMELINE

[ AUG - DEC 2024 ]

OVERVIEW

During this industry project, I designed a website that helps female solo travellers connect with others, find local events, get recommendations and learn more about staying safe and having fun. As the researcher, UX/UI designer and front-end developer, I led the end-to-end research and design process for the platform, encompassing user research, site maps, and design development in Figma and front-end development using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Research insights were synthesised through thematic analysis and affinity mapping to identify key safety concerns, social barriers, and motivations shaping the experiences of female solo travellers.

The final platform balanced accessibility requirements by adhering to WCAG guidelines and was guided by Mike Monteiro’s Designer’s Code of Ethics, ensuring that safety, consent, and user trust were prioritised throughout the experience. The design focused on creating a supportive and transparent environment that empowered users to connect, share knowledge, and make informed decisions while travelling independently.

Tools & Methods: Figma, UX Research, Thematic Analysis, Affinity Mapping, User Flows, Site Maps, Prototyping

THE ASK

Female solo travelers often face challenges finding reliable information, safety guidance, and like-minded peers while traveling, making it harder to feel supported and connected.

THE DESIGN

Created a community-driven web platform where users can share experiences, access safety tips, discover events, and connect with other travelers. The platform fosters friendship, knowledge sharing, and confidence, helping solo travelers feel supported throughout their journeys.

RESEARCH SYNTHESIS & INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Through thematic analysis and affinity mapping, I synthesised qualitative research into clear patterns, identifying what users value most and where their concerns lie. Clustering insights into themes allowed me to prioritise opportunities and define design directions grounded in real user needs.

Building on these insights, I developed a site map to translate research into structure. The site map clarified core features, defined the platform’s purpose, and ensured each interaction aligned directly with user goals. This step established a strong foundation before moving into wireframing and interface design.

ETHICS DRIVEN DESIGN DECISIONS

Mike Monteiro’s Designer’s Code of Ethics informed the design and development of this platform at every stage. Each principle was used as a reflective framework to identify ethical risks, areas for refinement, and potential impacts on user safety, privacy, and trust. This process guided decisions around data collection, clarifying what information was necessary, how it would be used, and when it could be avoided altogether. Mitigation strategies, such as, minimising personal data, increasing transparency, and prioritising user control were embedded into the design to ensure the platform supported connection and empowerment without compromising user safety.

IDEATION & DEVELOPMENT

Using the site map as a foundation, I developed wireframes in Figma that progressively evolved into a high-fidelity, interactive prototype. This phase focused on translating research insights into clear, usable interface structures that supported safety, clarity, and community engagement.

Key Design Focus Areas

• Information clarity and content separation

• Visual hierarchy and emphasis

• Recognition over recall

• Consistent interaction patterns

PAGE STRUCTURE & EVOLUTION

Initial designs focused on six core pages: Emergency Response, Guides, Community, Events, Contact, and About Us. Through iterative refinement, these evolved into Home, Safety Guides, Events, Community, and Insights & Recommendations. This restructuring ensured each page provided a clear overview of its content while surfacing only the most relevant information, reducing clutter and improving overall usability.

The platform was built as a community-focused website. Iterative testing with target users informed refinements to ensure the experience felt safe, engaging, and supportive.

THE TAKEAWAY

This project strengthened my skills in designing for community and social connection. I learned how to create digital spaces that balance safety, engagement, and usability, and how thoughtful design can foster meaningful interactions and support among diverse users.
